# Vacation and Leave Policy

Leaves of absence and vacation may be granted to residents at the discretion of the Program Director in accordance with local policy. However, the total of such vacation and leaves for any reason — including, but not limited to, vacation, job interviews or sick leave, maternity or paternity leave, personal leave (such as attendance of additional conferences beyond the protected amount) — must comply with the American Board of Surgery graduation requirements. This includes 48 weeks of clinical service per year which may be averaged over the first 3 years (144 weeks) and the last 2 years (96 weeks).

In order for any absence to be approved, a formal request must be made to the program and flights/accommodations should not be booked until after the leave request is formally approved. The program will not be responsible for any cost incurred if leave cannot be approved and expenses have already been paid by the resident.

Furthermore, leave will not be granted if a resident has not turned in the required statistics at the end of each rotation and maintained a minimum compliance with duty hour logs.

## Vacation

**Annual Vacation Allowance:** Residents receive four (4) weeks of non-clinical time per academic year, defined as 20 weekdays. This includes:

- Three (3) weeks of vacation (15 weekdays)
- One (1) flexible week (5 weekdays) that may be used for:
  - Conferences
  - Fellowship interviews
  - Additional vacation time
- All vacation periods will include at least two weekend days on one end of the vacation period. Bookend weekends on both sides of the vacation are not guaranteed, but residents may request them through their service chief and they will be accommodated when possible.
  - Weekend days associated with vacation will count toward the ACGME requirement of four days off per month.

## Vacation Scheduling and Approval

Vacation scheduling may be affected by clinical service coverage requirements and dates must be approved in advance of any travel plans being made. Priority for vacation approval will be given to the earliest request. When simultaneous, priority will be given to the more senior resident making the request. Any other situations will be resolved at the discretion of the program director.

Residents may use vacation flexibly throughout the year. If a resident wishes to use a larger portion of vacation in a given year or borrow vacation time from another year (for example for fellowship interviews or conferences), this may be permitted provided that the resident remains compliant with American Board of Surgery (ABS) clinical service requirements. Vacation may be taken:

- As full weeks, or
- As individual weekdays when used for interviews or conferences.

**Exceptions:** The following do not count toward vacation or time away:

- Scheduled holiday blocks
- Professional development courses for which the resident is nominated by the program
- Approved conferences (see conference policy)

## Vacation Restrictions

To maintain safe clinical coverage, the following restrictions apply:

- **No two residents may be absent from the same service at the same time.** The following services are considered single rotations for vacation purposes:
  - Surgical Oncology: HPB, Melanoma/Sarcoma, Breast, Endocrine
  - East Services: East General Surgery, East ACS, East Vascular
- **No two residents may be absent from the same call pool at the same time.**

### Call Pools

| Call Pool | Services |

| East Call Pool | *(not specified)* |

- **Two weeks of vacation may not be from the same service/block** unless separated by at least 4 clinical weeks of service, unless special approval is obtained.
- **Back-to-back vacation weeks are not permitted** unless:
  - The weeks occur on two different services, or
  - The resident is in the final two weeks of the chief year, or
  - The request is specifically approved by the Service Education Liaison, Administrative Chief, and Program Leadership.
- **Vacation Blackout Periods:** Vacation is not permitted during:
  - Block 1 (July)
  - Block 7
  - Block 13 (June)
- Vacation may not be combined with holiday blocks.
- The following rotations do not permit vacation:
  - Night float (all PGY levels)
  - Intern Simulation rotation

## Outside/Off-Service Rotators

- Vacation is not permitted for outside rotators on their 1-month transplant surgery block.
- All off-service rotators are asked to comply with all department of surgery vacation policies for vacation requests occurring during time on general surgery services.
- All outside rotators and off-service residents must have vacation reviewed and approved by the general surgery program before it is granted.
- Failure to obtain approval may result in the inability to support future vacation requests while rotating on general surgery services.

## Exceptions

Requests that fall outside these restrictions may be considered on a case-by-case basis and require approval from:

- The Service Education Liaison
- The Administrative Chief
- Program Leadership

Residents on the vacation block schedule (categorical general surgery PGY-1 and PGY-2) are exempt from these restrictions, as coverage for their absences is built into the schedule.
